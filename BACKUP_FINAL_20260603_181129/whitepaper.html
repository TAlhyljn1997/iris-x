⚖️ 3. Seven Balance Laws™ (Complete Theory & Examples)

3.1 Liquidity Law (L)

Definition: Measures execution depth and market absorption capacity.
L = Volume₃₀ₘ / Volatility_intraday × (Bid-Ask Spread / P_mid)

Range: 0–100

  • 0–30: Illiquid (thin order book)
  • 31–60: Moderate
  • 61–100: Highly liquid (deep book)

Example: PLS

Liquidity = 46 → Below average, expect slippage on large orders

3.2 Flow Law (F)

Definition: Tracks institutional vs retail order flow direction.
F = ((Institutional Volume / Total Volume) - 0.5) × 200 + Delta Bias

Range: –100 to +100

  • < –50: Strong distribution
  • –50 to +50: Neutral / Rotation
  • > +50: Strong accumulation

Example: PLS

Flow = +89 → Strong institutional accumulation, retail selling being absorbed

3.3 Risk Law (R)

Definition: Volatility regime and price uncertainty.
R = √(Σ(P_i - P̄) / P̄) × 100

Annualized Volatility: R × √252

  • 0–25: Low (<20%)
  • 26–50: Moderate (20–40%)
  • 51–75: High (40–60%)
  • 76–100: Extreme (>60%)

Example: PLS

Risk = –51 → Moderate volatility ≈40% annualized, controlled environment

3.4 Time Law (T)

Definition: Price alignment with moving average cycles.
  • Close > SMA₅₀ > SMA₂₀₀ = 100 (strong uptrend)
  • Close near SMA₂₀ = 50 (transition)
  • Close < SMA₂₀ = 0 (downtrend)

Example: PLS

Time = +80 → Close > all SMAs, trend confirmation

3.5 Possession Law (P)

Definition: Ownership shift from retail to institutional.
P = ((Cum Vol_Inst - Cum Vol_Retail) / Total Vol) × 100
  • < –50: Retail dominance
  • –50 to +50: Balanced
  • > +50: Institutional dominance

Example: PLS

Possession = +100 → Complete institutional control, late accumulation phase

3.6 Impact Law (I)

Definition: Price response to volume and execution pressure.
I = (ΔPrice_session × Price_base / Volume_total) × 100
  • 0–30: Low (large volume, small move)
  • 31–60: Moderate
  • 61–100: High (aggressive orders)

Example: PLS

Impact = –100 → Negative short-term but being absorbed by institutions

3.7 Authority Law (A)

Definition: Venue dominance and market control.
A = (Volume_ASX / Volume_Total × 100) - (Volume_DarkPool / Volume_Total × 25)
  • 0–40: Fragmented
  • 41–70: Moderate concentration
  • 71–100: Strong dominance

Example: PLS

Authority = +84 → ASX dominant (95%), transparent price discovery

3.8 Balance Index (Composite Score)

Balance Index = Avg(L, F, R, T, P, I, A)
Balance Index Market Health Action

60–74 Good HOLD / SELECTIVE

🎛️ 4. Tape Engine (Complete Analysis)

4.1 What is the Tape?

The "Tape" is the real-time order flow — a reconstruction of executed trades showing who traded, at what price, and in what volume. It answers: "Who's really trading? At what prices? In what quantities?"

4.2 Key Tape Metrics

VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)

VWAP = Σ(P_i × V_i) / Σ V_i

Meaning: The true fair value where the market traded.

  • Close > VWAP → Buyers more aggressive
  • Close < VWAP → Sellers more aggressive

Volume Profile

Shows: How much volume traded at each price level.

  • Highest volume = Point of Control (POC) = fair value
  • POC is next day's support
  • Concentrated POC = institutional accumulation zone

CVD (Cumulative Volume Delta)

CVD_t = Σ(V_buy - V_sell)

Meaning: Running tally of buy vs sell pressure.

  • CVD > 0 and rising → buyers winning
  • CVD < 0 and falling → sellers winning

Block Trades (>5K shares)

Shows: Institutional activity.

  • Block % >70% → institutional dominance
  • Blocks at high prices = accumulation
  • Blocks at low prices = distribution

Example: PLS Tape Analysis

VWAP: $6.7534 (below close $6.79) → Buyers aggressive

Volume Profile POC: $6.77 → Institutional accumulation zone

CVD: +82K → Buy pressure slightly stronger

Block Trades: 76.7% of volume → Institutional control

📊 5. Volume Spike Theory (RI‑Ecosys™)

5.1 What is a Volume Spike?

A volume spike occurs when daily volume exceeds the 20-day rolling mean by ≥1.5 standard deviations.

VSI = (V_t - μ_V) / σ_V ≥ 1.5

5.2 Three Interpretations

Case A: Price UP + High Volume

Interpretation: Institutional Accumulation

  • Flow Law > +50
  • Impact Law ≥ 0
  • VWAP < Close

Action: BUY / ACCUMULATE

Case B: Price DOWN + High Volume

Interpretation: Institutional Distribution

  • Flow Law < –50
  • Impact Law < 0
  • VWAP > Close

Action: REDUCE / EXIT

Case C: Mixed Signals + High Volume

Interpretation: Rotation Phase

  • Flow Law between –30 and +30
  • Impact Law oscillating
  • VWAP near Close

Action: HOLD / WAIT FOR CLARITY

Tape & Flow Terminology

VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)
The true fair value where the market traded. Calculated as Σ(Price × Volume) / Σ Volume.
Volume Profile
Distribution of volume across price levels. Point of Control (POC) = highest volume level = next day's support.
CVD (Cumulative Volume Delta)
Running tally of buy volume minus sell volume. Positive = buyers winning, Negative = sellers winning.
Block Trade
Institutional trade ≥ 1,000 shares or ≥5,000 shares (depending on definition). Used to detect institutional activity.
Institutional Flow
Proportion of volume coming from institutional participants (typically >1K shares per trade).
Dark Pool Activity
Off-exchange trading (non-transparent). Higher dark pool activity may indicate stealth distribution.
